The world is burning. The water flooded the tracks and I couldn’t get home. Some people died in their basements. I packed up all of my belongings and left for a town in another state. It usually takes 2 hours but today it took 5 because everyone was leaving. My neighborhood will never be the same. It is gone from me. I froze water bottles to keep my food cold and filled the sinks and bathtubs with water in case we run out. I can’t escape the hurricane’s path. The cities by us are burning. Are we next? When I go hiking, I see the large swaths of yellow hills. “HIGH FIRE RISK / DROUGHT,” say the signs on the trail. I think about who lived there before us, about Indigenous sovereignty for the land, and how removing them from this land has brought us here. I feel sick. I went to school the next day.
